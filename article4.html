Nutrition and Health: Foods that slow down aging

Varich Journal • August 17, 2024

Aging is an inevitable part of life, influenced by many factors such as lifestyle, environment, and genetics. However, the primary causes of aging are the accumulation of cellular damage caused by reactive molecules known as free radicals, and the shortening of telomeres — structures located at the ends of chromosomes that play a key role in cell division.

Free radicals are unstable molecules formed in the body due to various factors such as UV radiation, environmental pollution, poor nutrition, or stress. They have unpaired electrons and seek to "steal" electrons from other molecules, damaging cells and causing oxidative stress. Oxidative stress leads to premature cellular aging, functional decline, and the development of various diseases, including cancer, cardiovascular disorders, and neurodegenerative conditions.

Cell division is the process by which one cell divides into two new cells. It is crucial for growth, tissue repair, and overall health maintenance. During cell division, DNA is copied so each new cell receives a full set of genetic information. However, with each division, the telomeres at the ends of chromosomes become shorter. Once telomeres become too short, cells lose the ability to divide, leading to aging and eventual cell death. This is one of the key biological processes driving aging.

While aging cannot be stopped, it is possible to make dietary and lifestyle changes that slow down certain aging processes and reduce the risk of age-related diseases. This article presents a selection of foods that can help slow down aging.

  1. Turmeric. The main active compound in turmeric, curcumin, has powerful cell-protective properties due to its strong antioxidant effects. Turmeric activates certain proteins that help slow down cellular aging and promote longevity. This has been confirmed in animal studies, which showed that curcumin combats cellular damage and significantly increases lifespan. The compound has also been shown to delay the onset of age-related diseases and alleviate their symptoms.
  2. Collagen. This popular dietary supplement may help prevent skin aging by boosting collagen levels in the skin. As we age, the production of collagen — a protein that helps maintain skin structure — slows down, leading to visible signs of aging such as wrinkles. Some studies suggest that collagen supplementation can reduce signs of aging, including wrinkles and dryness, and improve skin elasticity.
  3. Vitamin C. This powerful antioxidant helps protect cells from oxidative damage, supports immune function, regulates inflammation, and promotes healthy aging. Vitamin C also enhances concentration, memory, decision-making, and recognition ability in older adults. Additionally, it is essential for skin health: it improves hydration, stimulates collagen production, and protects against wrinkles and premature aging caused by sun exposure.
  4. Coenzyme Q10 (CoQ10). This antioxidant is naturally produced in the body and plays a vital role in energy production and cellular protection. As we age, CoQ10 levels decline, which can lead to reduced mental and physical performance. CoQ10 supplements may benefit heart health by reducing arterial stiffness, lowering blood pressure, and preventing the buildup of oxidized cholesterol in the arteries.
  5. Epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG). This polyphenol compound found in green tea offers numerous health benefits, including reducing the risk of certain cancers and heart disease. EGCG also supports longevity and protects against age-related diseases by restoring mitochondrial function in cells and influencing aging processes. Additionally, it stimulates autophagy—a natural process through which the body clears out damaged cellular components.
  6. Vitamin E. It plays a key role in immune function and the regulation of inflammation. As a powerful antioxidant, it protects cells from oxidative stress and helps prevent cellular damage. Vitamin E can be obtained from various foods, including plant oils, almonds, hazelnuts, spinach, avocado, kiwi, and fish.
  7. Theanine. L-theanine is an amino acid found in certain teas, especially green tea. Studies show that L-theanine supplements may help improve brain function in middle-aged adults and protect against age-related liver dysfunction, oxidative stress, and inflammation.
  8. Fisetin. This flavonoid compound is considered senolytic, meaning it has the ability to eliminate old (senescent) cells. Studies on rodents have shown that fisetin can reduce the number of aged cells in tissues, extend lifespan, and protect the brain from age-related changes.
  9. Sulforaphane. This sulfur - containing compound is found in cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and is known for its powerful anti-inflammatory properties. Recent studies have shown that sulforaphane can extend the lifespan of roundworms and prevent age-related heart issues in mice.
  10. Resveratrol. This polyphenol antioxidant is found in grapes, berries, peanuts, and red wine, and may promote longevity by activating certain enzymes called sirtuins. Animal studies suggest that resveratrol can extend lifespan; however, more high-quality research in humans is needed.

P.S. In this article, I’ve highlighted foods that can significantly contribute to slowing down the aging process. Of course, there are many other healthy foods that positively affect overall health. However, there are also other factors and nuances that deserve attention in a separate article. For example, it’s important to remember that excessive consumption of green tea or foods high in iron can be harmful, as iron may promote the formation of free radicals, leading to cell damage and premature aging. That’s why it’s essential to follow the principle: eat healthy foods, but in moderation.
