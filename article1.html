Sleep and Nutrition: Which Products Help You Get a Good Sleep

Varich Journal â€¢ September 30, 2023

Sleep is an extremely important aspect of our life, which significantly affects physical and mental health, as well as general well-being.

How long does it take to sleep? It is usually recommended to sleep for 7-9 hours every night, but individual needs may vary, and you can choose the duration of sleep that suits you.

Consequences of lack of sleep and insomnia. Insufficient sleep and insomnia can cause a number of serious health problems, including an increased risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes, obesity, impaired brain function and mental disorders.

Nutrients, Antioxidants and Hormones Affect Sleep. Magnesium (promotes muscle relaxation), boron, calcium, potassium, vitamin D, omega-3 fatty acids, folic acid, antioxidants such as anthocyanins and flavonoids (helps fight stress). Tryptophan is an amino acid that helps produce the hormones melatonin (sleep hormone) and serotonin (happiness hormone), improving sleep quality.

In this article we will consider. Foods and drinks that can help improve sleep quality, help overcome insomnia and reduce the risk of developing chronic diseases. It is important to remember that it is best to eat these foods 1-3 hours before going to bed, as eating food right before going to bed can cause digestive problems.

Main dishes

  1. Turkey
  2. Fatty Fish

Turkey contains protein and several vitamins and minerals, such as riboflavin and phosphorus. It is also rich in the amino acid tryptophan, which promotes the production of melatonin. Fatty fish such as salmon, tuna, trout and mackerel are extremely beneficial due to their vitamin D content and omega-3 fatty acids, particularly EPA and DHA. The combination of omega-3 fatty acids and vitamin D in oily fish can have a positive effect on sleep quality, as both have been shown to promote serotonin production. Eating a few grams of turkey or fatty fish before bed can help you fall asleep faster and sleep more deeply, including fewer awakenings during the night.

Porridge

  1. Oatmeal
  2. White rice

White rice and oatmeal are high in carbohydrates and limited in fiber, which can cause drowsiness if consumed at least 1 hour before bedtime. This is due to their high glycemic index (GI), which indicates how quickly blood sugar levels rise after a meal. A 79-gram serving of white rice can provide your daily needs for folate and thiamin. Oatmeal also contains melatonin, which can positively affect your sleep.

Dairy products

  1. Milk
  2. Cheese
  3. Yogurt

Dairy products are known sources of tryptophan. Milk has been shown to improve sleep in the elderly, especially when combined with light exercise.

Fruits

  1. Kiwi
  2. Bananas

Kiwi is a low-calorie fruit that contains only 42 calories, as well as a significant amount of nutrients such as vitamin K, folate, potassium and serotonin. Kiwifruit can help improve your digestion and help lower blood cholesterol. Consuming two kiwis an hour before bedtime can lead to positive results. Bananas contain magnesium, and banana peels contain tryptophan. These properties can help improve your sleep and provide you with quality rest.

Nuts

  1. Almond
  2. Walnuts

Roasted almonds and walnuts are sources of numerous nutrients, including magnesium, phosphorus, riboflavin, manganese and copper. Regular consumption of 28 grams of these nuts may help reduce the risk of certain chronic diseases, such as type 2 diabetes and heart disease, thanks to their monounsaturated fat, fiber and antioxidants. In addition, walnuts are rich in omega-3 fatty acids and linoleic acid, which contribute to the production of serotonin. Both of these nuts also improve sleep quality as they are among the best sources of melatonin.

Hot drinks

  1. Chamomile tea
  2. Green tea
  3. Lavender tea

A series of teas can prove effective in reducing stress, fatigue, symptoms of depression and anxiety by functioning as an effective sedative. Drinking a cup of tea before bed can help you form good habits to improve sleep quality. The brain reacts to this habit as preparation for sleep. This approach should be considered as an additional tool for solving sleep problems, not as a one-size-fits-all solution to all sleep problems.

Cold drinks

  1. Cherry juice (Tart)

A tasty and healthy drink that improves the quality of your sleep and prevents insomnia thanks to its high content of melatonin, promoting sleepiness and influencing dreams. Only 240 ml of this drink provides the necessary amount of magnesium, phosphorus and potassium. In addition, it is rich in antioxidants such as anthocyanins and flavonols, which support overall health.

Advice

  • Try to stick to a regular eating schedule and avoid eating very large dinners before bed.
  • Caffeine & alcohol can interfere with quality sleep. Avoid taking them a few hours before bed.
  • Drinking enough water throughout the day is important for healthy sleep.
  • Don't overeat before bed. Avoid excessive intake of fat and sugar as this may cause discomfort.
  • Before bed, eat foods that can help your blood sugar levels stay stable.
